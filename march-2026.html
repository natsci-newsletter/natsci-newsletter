National Updates on AI in STEM
Federal agencies are launching national initiatives that position AI as a core tool for scientific discovery. These programs are expanding access to AI infrastructure and accelerating research across energy, climate science, engineering, and other STEM fields.
National Artificial Intelligence Research Resource (NAIRR)
The National Artificial Intelligence Research Resource (NAIRR) pilot continues expanding nationwide access to advanced AI tools, computing resources, and datasets for researchers and educators. Led by the National Science Foundation, the program connects universities, nonprofits, and startups with shared infrastructure needed to conduct cutting-edge AI research. By democratizing access to high-performance computing and AI models, NAIRR supports work across climate science, medicine, engineering, and materials research.
Learn more
Department of Energy’s Genesis Mission
In 2026, the U.S. Department of Energy announced 26 science and technology challenges under its Genesis Mission, an initiative using artificial intelligence and advanced computing to accelerate scientific discovery. The program integrates AI with research at U.S. national laboratories to analyze large datasets, run complex simulations, and design new experiments. These efforts focus on areas including energy systems, nuclear science, climate modeling, and advanced materials.

Ed Tech Teaching Tip
This month’s strategy turns AI outputs into opportunities for scientific analysis.
EdTech Teaching Tip: Turn AI Into a Scientific “Error Hunt”
AI can produce fluent explanations, but they aren’t always correct. Inspired by guidance from the MIT Teaching + Learning Lab, instructors can use AI outputs as material for analysis rather than answers.

Try this simple activity:
• Ask an AI tool to generate an explanation or solution for a concept from your course.
• Prompt the AI to include 2–3 mistakes or misconceptions.
• Share the output with students.
• Ask students to identify the errors and explain the correct reasoning.

Because AI responses can sound convincing even when flawed, this activity encourages students to carefully evaluate logic, assumptions, and evidence; skills that are central to scientific thinking.

Source: MIT Teaching + Learning Lab: Applying the Science of Learning in Your Teaching: Generative AI May Help.
